Career Outlook

A hockey head coach is responsible for overseeing all aspects of a team's performance, including strategy development, player development, and game management. They serve as the leader, motivator, and strategist, ensuring their team is prepared both mentally and physically for each game. They go around to help at different ice rinks and work with players of all ages to develop them into the best player they can be. They help create players to play professional hockey or play professional hockey at the highest level possible.

The salary of a general coach is about $45,910 per year. The degree typical for a coach is a bachelor's degree, and there are over 275,000 working in the field. You can view more information about the salary and job outlook here. You get to work with many different hockey players, some who may even have ties to the NHL, or other big name leagues. You need to have knowledge of the game and be able to teach players using drills related to the game along with keeping them all in check and continue their development.

Celebrities In The Field

Fans think highly of professional hockey coaches. You can view a list of the fan-voted greatest coaches of all time here. Some of the greats include: Scotty Bowman, a canadian NHL coach who holds the record for the most wins in the regular season and playoffs by a coach; Herb Brooks, coach of the USA olympic team at Lake Placid, where his team defeated the Russians in what would become known as the "Miracle On Ice"; and Al Arbour, a legendary coach for the New York Islanders, who brought them 4 consecutive Stanley Cup titles.
